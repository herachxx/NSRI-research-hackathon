Event Overview

A structured research sprint, not a lecture series

The event is built around short live sessions, team execution time, and focused feedback from mentors and judges.

Program rhythm

Teams go from topic selection to a polished research paper with checkpoints every evening.

Participants join a fully virtual sprint on Zoom, work in teams of one to four, choose one of six research categories, and build toward a PDF research submission. No prior research experience is required; the format is designed to make research methods, writing, feedback, and presentation practice approachable.

Judging Process

Two rounds, three outcomes

Every submission is read. Only the strongest move forward at each stage.

01Jul 19-24

Initial Screening

Track judges review all submissions against the official rubric and shortlist the strongest projects for the top 100.

02Jul 19-24

Expert Review

Expert and associate judges review the top 100, finalize the top 25, and prepare scorecards, finale order, and live Q&A flow.

03Jul 25

Grand Finale

The top 25 teams present live on Zoom. The final panel completes Q&A, final scoring, winners, and closing remarks.

Panel structure

Final review uses a five-judge panel with engineering, product, research, and domain expertise. Judges do not score teams they mentored.

Tie-breakers

Ties are resolved by Impact score, then Technical Execution, then a judge vote if needed.

Feedback

All teams receive score sheets within 72 hours after final judging. People's Choice voting is limited to verified ticket holders.

Evaluation Criteria

Four equal categories, scored out of 120

The rubric rewards original thinking, credible execution, real-world relevance, and clear communication. Each category is worth 30 points.

Total120
30 pts

Innovation & Creativity

Originality of the question, approach, and insight relative to the chosen research area.

30 pts

Technical Execution

Strength of method, evidence, analysis, implementation, and rigor behind the final submission.

30 pts

Impact & Relevance

How clearly the project connects to a meaningful problem, audience, field, or practical use case.

30 pts

Presentation & Communication

Clarity of the paper, visuals, narrative, assumptions, limitations, and final presentation.

Tie-breakers: Impact score, then Technical Execution, then judge vote if still needed.

How It Runs

The tools behind the sprint

Team coordination

Once teams are formed, you'll coordinate directly over whatever channel you already use - WhatsApp, Discord, or email.

Live sessions

The opening ceremony, mentorship sessions, and the Grand Finale all run on Zoom, with links sent to every registered participant.

Submissions

Teams submit one PDF research paper through a shared Google Form. Tech tracks are encouraged to add a GitHub repo and a three-minute video walkthrough.

Support

A dedicated inbox stays open for the full event for technical issues, rule questions, and anything else that comes up.

About NSRI

The National Student Research Institute (NSRI) is a student-governed 501(c)(3) nonprofit dedicated to democratizing access to research opportunities for students worldwide.

Founded on the belief that research experience shouldn't be limited by geography, institution, or background, NSRI has built a global community of 16,000+ aspiring researchers across more than 40 countries - through research challenges, webinars, and training programs designed and run by students who understand the barriers firsthand.

Introduce

Students learn the research process through accessible, structured events.

Build

Teams create portfolio-worthy projects for college and career applications.

Connect

Emerging researchers meet mentors, reviewers, publication pathways, and professional networks.
